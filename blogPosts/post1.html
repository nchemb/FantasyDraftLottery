Why Play Fantasy Football?

Updated January 20, 2025 by FDL Team

What Is Fantasy Football?

Fantasy football is a game where you build a virtual roster of real NFL players and compete against other managers based on those players' real-game statistics. You draft your team at the start of the season, manage your roster week to week, and try to outscore your opponents. It runs alongside the NFL regular season, and every Sunday becomes a lot more interesting when you have skin in the game.

Why People Play Fantasy Football

The short answer: it makes football more fun. Here is the longer answer.

It Gives You a Stake in Every Game

When you have a running back on your roster, you suddenly care about games that have nothing to do with your favorite team. A Monday night matchup between two teams you would normally skip becomes must-watch TV when your running back is getting carries. Fantasy football turns 15 games a week into appointment viewing.

It Builds Competition Within Your Social Circle

The best fantasy leagues are the ones you run with friends, family, or coworkers. A 10 or 12 team league creates a season-long competition that gives everyone something to talk about, trash talk about, and celebrate. The social layer is what keeps people coming back year after year, often more than the football itself.

It Deepens Your Knowledge of the Sport

To compete in fantasy football, you need to understand player roles, matchups, offensive schemes, and injury reports. Over time, most fantasy players develop a much deeper understanding of how the NFL actually works. If you want to learn football, playing fantasy is one of the fastest ways to do it.

Draft Day Is an Event

The draft is the highlight of the fantasy football calendar. Whether you do it in person or online, draft day brings the whole league together. Picks are made, trades are proposed, and the season officially begins. Many leagues turn draft day into a full event with food, drinks, and a live draft order reveal using a tool like Fantasy Draft Lottery.

How to Determine Your Draft Order Fairly

Before the draft, every league needs to figure out who picks where. The fairest way is a randomized lottery. Some leagues use a weighted system where last year's worst teams get better odds at the top pick, similar to how the NBA draft lottery works. You can run a random or weighted draft lottery for free at fantasydraftlottery.com in under a minute, with support for 2 to 32 teams.

Is Fantasy Football Right for You?

If you enjoy the NFL, want a reason to stay engaged all season, and have a group of friends or coworkers who are interested, then yes. It takes about 30 minutes a week to manage your roster well. The investment is low and the entertainment value is high.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is fantasy football worth playing?

Yes. It gives you a personal stake in every NFL game, makes watching more engaging, and builds social connections through league competition. Most players find it deepens their enjoyment of the sport significantly.

How does a fantasy football league work?

Each manager drafts a roster of real NFL players. Each week, your players score points based on their real-game statistics. You compete head-to-head against another manager in your league, and the team with the most points wins that week.

How do you determine draft order for a fantasy football league?
